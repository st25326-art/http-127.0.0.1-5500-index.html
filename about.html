About McDonald's

Our story and what makes us iconic

Our History

McDonald's was founded in 1940 by brothers Richard and Maurice McDonald in California. Originally, it was a small drive-in restaurant, but they quickly realised the need for a faster and more efficient way to serve customers.

In 1948, they introduced the "Speedee Service System", which focused on preparing food quickly and consistently. This system became the foundation of modern fast-food service and allowed customers to receive their meals in a matter of minutes.

In 1955, businessman Ray Kroc joined the company and helped expand McDonald's into a global franchise. His vision transformed the brand into one of the most recognised and successful fast-food chains in the world.

Today, McDonald's operates in over 100 countries, serving millions of customers every day. It continues to evolve by introducing new menu items, improving technology, and focusing on customer experience.
